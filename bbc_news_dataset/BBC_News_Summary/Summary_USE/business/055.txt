Economist Colen Garrow said the budget looked as if it would stimulate economic growth. Aiming to both stir economic growth and aid the country's poor, finance minister Trevor Manuel said the focus of the 2005 budget was "more for all". Unveiling the 418bn-rand budget to parliament, Mr Manuel said the South African economy had grown by an average of 3.2% over the past four years, slightly below the African average of 4%. Spending on health and education will rise by 9.4% and 8.1% respectively. Spending on housing and sanitation will rise by 12%.
