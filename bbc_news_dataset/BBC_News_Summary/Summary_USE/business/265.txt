Troubled Marsh under SEC scrutinyThe US stock market regulator is investigating troubled insurance broker Marsh & McLennan's shareholder transactions, the firm has said. Prosecutors allege that Marsh - the world's biggest insurance broker - and other US insurance firms may have fixed bids for corporate cover. The SEC's investigation into so-called related party transactions includes dealings in the Trident Funds, managed by MMC Capital, the company's private equity firm. Marsh is also the focus of an inquiry the New York attorney-general into whether insurers rigged the market.
