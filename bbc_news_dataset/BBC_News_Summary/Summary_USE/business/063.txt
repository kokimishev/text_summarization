Excluding the car sector, US retail sales were up 0.6% in January, twice what some analysts had been expecting. Excluding the car sector, sales rose by just 0.3%. Sales at appliance and electronic stores were down 0.6% in January, while sales at hardware stores dropped by 0.3% and furniture store sales dipped 0.1%. Cars pull down US retail figuresUS retail sales fell 0.3% in January, the biggest monthly decline since last August, driven down by a heavy fall in car sales. In December, overall retail sales rose by 1.1%. US retail spending is expected to rise in 2005, but not as quickly as in 2004.
