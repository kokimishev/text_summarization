Using the most internationally-accepted methodology of the International Labour Organisation (ILO), Germany had 3.97 million people out of work in January. The soaring official unemployment figure follows a change in the methodology which pushed up the jobless rate by more than 500,000 in January. German jobless rate at new recordMore than 5.2 million Germans were out of work in February, new figures show. The latest unemployment figures look set to increase the pressure on the government. The figure of 5.216 million people, or 12.6% of the working-age population, is the highest jobless rate in Europe's biggest economy since the 1930s. And ILO-based figures also suggest that 14,000 new net jobs were created that month, taking the number of people employed to 38.9 million.
