Meanwhile, economists cautioned against reading too much into data from the Federal Reserve showing an unexpected $8.7bn drop in consumer debt in November. Steady job growth continues in USThe US created fewer jobs than expected in December, but analysts said that the dip in hiring was not enough to derail the world's biggest economy. The unemployment rate stayed at 5.4% in December, and about 200,000 jobs will need to be created each month if that figure is to drop. While worries still remain, the conditions are set for steady growth in 2005, analysts said. Job creation was one of last year's main concerns for the US economy. The Fed lifted borrowing costs five times last year to 2.25%, citing evidence the US economic recovery was becoming more robust.
