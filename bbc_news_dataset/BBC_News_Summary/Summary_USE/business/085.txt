Mortgage lending rose by £7.1bn in December, up from a £6.4bn rise in November. Changes to mortgage regulation may have artificially depressed figures in November, thus flattering the December figures, analysts said. "The modest rise in mortgage approvals and lending in December reinforces the impression that the housing market is currently slowing steadily rather than sharply," said Global Insight analyst Howard Archer, commenting on the BoE's figures. Home loan approvals rising againThe number of mortgages approved in the UK has risen for the first time since May last year, according to lending figures from the Bank of England.
