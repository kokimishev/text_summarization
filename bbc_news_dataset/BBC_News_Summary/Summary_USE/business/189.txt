The management changes are part of a wider shake-up of the business following Fiat's resolution of its dispute with General Motors. Mr Marchionne, who only joined the company last year, said Fiat Auto was now the "principal focus" of his attention. As part of a major restructuring, Fiat is to integrate the Maserati car company - currently owned by Ferrari - within its own operations. Sergio Marchionne has replaced Herbert Demel as chief executive of Fiat Auto, with Mr Demel leaving the company. Earlier this week, Fiat reached an agreement with GM to dissolve an alliance which could have obliged GM to buy the Italian firm outright. Fiat chief takes steering wheelThe chief executive of the Fiat conglomerate has taken day-to-day control of its struggling car business in an effort to turn it around. The car business, best known for its Alfa Romeo marque, is expected to make a loss of about 800m euros in 2004.
