Japan's economy grew by just 0.1% in the three months to September, according revised data issued this month. Nonetheless, Japanese firms have been stepping up capital investment, and the survey found the pace is quickening. In the Bank of Japan's December survey, the balance of big manufacturers saying business conditions are better, minus those saying they are worse, was 22, down from 26 in September. With the recovery slowing, the world's second biggest economy is now expected grow by 0.2% in 2004. December's confidence level was below that seen in September, the Bank of Japan said.
