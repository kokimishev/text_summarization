Nicolas Sarkozy of France said he and his colleagues were unanimous in their worry that the decline of the dollar would hit Europe's economies by eating into their exports. Meeting in Brussels, finance ministers of the 12 eurozone countries voiced their concern that the rise of the european currency was harming exports. Economists point out that whatever Europe says, in the short term a weaker dollar is a boon to President George W Bush's administration. And the European Union's monetary affairs commissioner, made it clear that action was necessary. Europe blames US over weak dollarEuropean leaders have openly blamed the US for the sharp rise in the value of the euro.
