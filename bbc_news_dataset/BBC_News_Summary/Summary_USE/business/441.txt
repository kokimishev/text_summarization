Overall UK exports - including both goods and services - rose by more than 3.2% to £24.8bn, although total imports rose again to a new record of £27.9bn. Non-EU imports rose 3%, but the growth in goods entering the UK from the EU slowed to 0.5%. November saw an improvement in export levels to both the European Union and the rest of the world, the Office for National Statistics (ONS) said. Non-EU exports shot up 7.5%, with growth seen across a range of manufacturing sectors including cars, consumer durables and chemicals. Trade gap narrows as exports riseThe UK's trade gap narrowed in November, helped by a 7.5% rise in exports outside the European Union. The country's surplus on trade-in-services remained steady at £1.5bn for the fifth month in a row.
