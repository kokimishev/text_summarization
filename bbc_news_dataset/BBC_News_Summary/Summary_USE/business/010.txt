Prosecutors wanted the cigarette firms to surrender $280bn in profits accumulated over the past 50 years and impose tougher rules on marketing their products. Court rejects $280bn tobacco caseA US government claim accusing the country's biggest tobacco companies of covering up the effects of smoking has been thrown out by an appeal court. The tobacco companies deny that they illegally conspired to promote smoking and defraud the public. Among the accused were Altria Group, RJ Reynolds Tobacco, Lorillard Tobacco, Liggett Group and Brown and Williamson.
