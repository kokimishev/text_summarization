Chinese wine tempts Italy's IllvaItaly's Illva Saronno has agreed to buy 33% of Changyu, the largest wine maker in China. The Italian liqueur maker will acquire the shares from the Yantai State Asset Management Bureau. Chinese wine sales are growing, the US Agriculture Department said, with wine sales in 2003 up 25% at 61.1bn yuan. Anheuser-Busch, Heineken and Scottish & Newcastle have all invested in the Chinese beer industry in the last two years and now Illva Saronno is betting on the Chinese wine market. The state agency will also sell 10% of its stake in Changyu to another overseas company, although it did not say who.
