The economy had stagnated throughout the 1990s, experiencing only brief spurts of expansion amid long periods in the doldrums. Output in the period grew just 0.1%, an annual rate of 0.3%. But high oil prices have knocked 0.2% off the growth rate, while the falling dollar means products shipped to the US are becoming relatively more expensive. "Exports slowed while capital spending became weaker," said Hiromichi Shirakawa, chief economist at UBS Securities in Tokyo. "The amber light is flashing."
