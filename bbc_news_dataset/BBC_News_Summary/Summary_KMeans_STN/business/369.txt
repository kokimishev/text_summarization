Beer giant swallows Russian firmBrewing giant Inbev has agreed to buy Alfa-Eco's stake in Sun Interbrew, Russia's second-largest brewer, for up to 259.7m euros ($353.3m; Â£183.75m). It employs 77,000 people, running operations in over 30 countries across the Americas, Europe and Asia Pacific. Inbev was formed in August 2004 when Belgium's Interbrew bought Brazilian brewer Ambev. The Leuven-based brewery said it would own 97.3% of the voting shares and 98.8% of the non-voting shares of Sun Interbrew. The deal is expected to be completed in the first quarter of 2005.
