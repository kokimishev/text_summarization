Stock market eyes Japan recoveryJapanese shares have ended the year at their highest level since 13 July amidst hopes of an economic recovery during 2005. In 2005 it "will rise toward 13,000", predicted Morgan Stanley equity strategist Naoki Kamiyama. Earlier this month, the quarterly Tankan survey of Japanese manufacturers found that business confidence had weakened for the first time since March 2003. Structural reform within Japan and an anticipated end to the banking sector's bad debt problems should also help, they say.
