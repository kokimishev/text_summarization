Venezuela - the world's fifth largest oil exporter - sells about 60% of its output to the United States. In 2004, according to forecasts from the Ministry of Commerce, China's oil imports will be 110m tons, up 21% on the previous year. Oil prices are finishing the year roughly 30% higher than they were in January 2004. Mr Chavez, who is visiting China, said his country would put its many of its oil facilities at the disposal of China. Venezuela has also offered to supply 120,000 barrels of fuel oil a month to China. Chinese firms would be allowed to operate 15 mature oil fields in the east of Venezuela, which could produce more than one billion barrels, he confirmed.
