However, firms continue to scale back investment in buildings and machinery. The CBI found some firms managed to increase prices for the first time in nine years - but many said increases failed to keep up the rise in costs. "Smaller manufacturers are facing an uphill struggle," said Hugh Morgan Williams, chair of the CBI's SME Council. "The manufacturing sector needs a period of long-term stability in the economy." When firms were questioned on output volume, the survey returned a balance of plus 8 - the highest rate of increase for seven years - and rose to plus 11 when looking ahead to the next three months.
