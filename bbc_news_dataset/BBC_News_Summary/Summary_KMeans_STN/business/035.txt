Pre-tax profits rose 15% from a year ago to reach £546m ($1.04bn), ahead of average analysts' forecasts of £532m. The company said the US Congressional elections and the FIFA World Cup are likely to present advertising opportunities in the near future. Chief Executive Martin Sorrell on Friday told Reuters news agency that WPP had submitted a proposal for United Business Media's NOP World market research unit. WPP in recent years has also bought firms such as Ogilvy & Mather and Cordiant Communications. The long-term outlook looks "very favourable" because of media and technology developments and the strength of the US economy, WPP said.
