Its index of national manufacturing activity rose to 58.6 last month from 57.8 in November. The ISM's index of national manufacturing activity is compiled from monthly responses of purchasing executives at more than 400 industrial companies, ranging from textiles to chemicals to paper, and has now been above 50 since June 2003. The result for December was slightly better than analysts' expectations and the 19th consecutive expansion. "While there is continuing upward pressure on prices, the rate of increase is slowing and definitely trending in the right direction." The ISM's "new orders" index rose to 67.4 from 61.5.
