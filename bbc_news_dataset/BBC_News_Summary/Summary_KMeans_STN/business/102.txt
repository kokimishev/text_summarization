US company admits Benin briberyA US defence and telecommunications company has agreed to pay $28.5m after admitting bribery in the West African state of Benin. The company says it no longer tolerates such practices. Prosecuting attorney Carol Lam said: "All US companies should take note that attempting to bribe foreign officials is criminal conduct and will be appropriately prosecuted."
