A slowdown in the housing market and this year's surge in oil prices has made economic forecasting all the more tricky, leading to a more uncertain outlook. The Bank of England is working in tandem with the ONS to improve the quality and speed of delivery of data. "The MPC judges that overall growth was a little higher in the third quarter than the official data currently indicate," Ms Lomax said. Rachel Lomax cited the late 1980s as an example of a time when weak economic figures were published, but substantially revised upwards years later.
