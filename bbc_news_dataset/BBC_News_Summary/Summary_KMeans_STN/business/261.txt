Booming markets shed few tearsThe market, former British government minister Michael Heseltine once said, has no morality. Indonesia is likely to have even lower take-up rates. In Indonesia, shares in state construction companies Adhi Karya and Semen Gresik have jumped sharply this week. But there are winners as well as losers. The MSCI World share index, a measure of global stock market performance, hit its highest level this week since early 2001; the BBC Global 30 has risen by 3% in the past week. The trillion-dollar debts of Japan's banks, for example, have had no effect on stock markets further afield than Tokyo. "It is not that the broking community is indifferent to disasters or feelings," one Bombay trader said this week. "But the reaction would have been seen if business had been affected. Business sense probably tends to overrule everything else."
